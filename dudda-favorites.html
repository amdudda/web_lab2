Movies

Tampopo
One of my favorite movies is Tampopo. It's often described as a "ramen western". It's a silly comedy that riffs off of the tropes of Cowboy Westerns, while also poking fun at Japanese culture. I watch it when I need a silly laugh.
Lilo and Stitch
A lovely children's movie about an orphaned girl and an evil alien who learn the power of chosen family.

Music

My favorite musician is Tanita Tikaram (website). She was a one-hit wonder back in the late 1980s, but has continued to produce albums at widely spaced intervals since then. It's hard to pick a favorite song, but two that I really love are "Wonderful Shadow" and "World Outside Your Window".

I also have delusions of being a musician. I used to play the cello, and I've taken up the tenor recorder. The picture below is one from Wikipedia, and it looks very annoying to play because it doesn't have an extension lever for low C. I would not enjoy having to stretch my little finger all the way down to reach that note! Mine only has a single lever for C, so I have no good way to play low C-sharp.

Books

I am a big fan of science fiction stories. Here's a few books I really enjoy.

  • Cyteen by C.J. Cherryh. It offers deep insight into the psychology of emotional manipulation.
  • The Foreigner series by C.J. Cherryh - a human diplomat navigates contradictory political demands while living amongst aliens whose supreme judicial arm is the Assassins' Guild.
  • The Xenogenesis trilogy by Octavia Butler. Just go read her stuff; it's really deep and looks hard at power, privilege, and oppression.
  • Little Sister by Kara Dalkey. This one is a short read, and fantasy rather than SF, but still a favorite.
  • For a fun romp, I recommend anything by Seanan McGuire. I suggest starting with Rosemary and Rue, which is basically "changeling kicks elvin ass in San Francisco". For a sampler of her style, she has some lovely short stories available for download at her website. Seanan also writes horror novels under the pseudonym Mira Grant.
